**The Signature Recipes & The Cellar (The Technical Specifications):**

1. **The Classic Margherita (The Purest Expression)**
* **The Ingredients:** 250g 48-hour cold-fermented dough (65% hydration), 80g hand-crushed San Marzano DOP tomatoes, 90g fresh Fior di Latte (drained for 4 hours), 4-5 fresh basil leaves, 5ml Extra Virgin Olive Oil (from Tuscany).
* **The Wine:** **Greco di Tufo (Campania, Italy)**.
* **The Pairing Logic:** To balance a Margherita, one must manage acidity. The San Marzano tomatoes have a distinct tang that requires a high-mineral, high-acidity white. The Greco di Tufo acts as a structural support; its flinty notes mimic the char of the wood-fired crust, while its clean finish prevents the mozzarella’s fats from coating the palate. It keeps the "system" refreshed.


2. **The Spicy Diavola (The Heat of the Forge)**
* **The Ingredients:** 250g dough, 80g tomato passata, 80g mozzarella, 60g thinly sliced Ventricina salami, 10g Calabrian chili oil, 15g grated Pecorino Romano, a pinch of dried oregano.
* **The Wine:** **Primitivo di Manduria (Puglia, Italy)**.
* **The Pairing Logic:** Capsaicin (heat) can "break" a delicate wine. You need a wine with bold fruit to act as a heat-shield. The Primitivo’s jammy dark fruit notes (blackberry, plum) provide a sweet counter-pressure to the spicy salami. The velvety tannins wrap around the chili oil, softening the "attack" on the tongue and turning a confrontation into a symphony.


3. **The Mythical Hawaiian (The Replicant’s Special)**
* **The Ingredients:** 250g dough, 70g light tomato base, 80g mozzarella, 50g Prosciutto Cotto (unsmoked), 60g fresh gold pineapple (caramelized in a pan for 2 mins with a touch of brown sugar), 10g fresh sliced jalapeños, 20g aged Provolone shavings.
* **The Wine:** **Off-dry German Riesling (Spätlese)**.
* **The Pairing Logic:** This pizza is a data-set of sweet (pineapple), salty (ham), and spicy (jalapeño). An off-dry Riesling is the only bridge. Its residual sugar tames the jalapeño’s fire and mirrors the pineapple’s sweetness, while its racing acidity cuts through the salt of the prosciutto and the funk of the provolone. It is a multi-layered solution for a complex pie.
